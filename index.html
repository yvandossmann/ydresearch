About Me

My main research interest is the understanding of environmental flows at fine scales, including, but not limited to, internal waves, turbulent mixing, gravity, and turbidity currents. I rely on the development and use of Eulerian and Lagrangian metrologies in the laboratory to describe oceanic and atmospheric processes. I use numerical modelling at the lab scale to complete and extend the parameter range of the experiments. I teach at Polytech'Nancy, mainly in the fields of fluid mechanics and optics.
